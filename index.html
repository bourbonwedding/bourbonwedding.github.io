Our Story

We first met on the dating website Tinder, when we both "swiped right" on April 15, 2015. Our first texts were a sign of things to come, full of silly jokes and Matt talking about geeky things.

Our first date was on April 17, 2015, at Untitled in Chicago. Over such delights as whiskey cocktails and deviled eggs, we shared laughs and serious admissions, and both knew we had found something significant.

Over the next few months, we experienced all kinds of dating adventures, including trips to Kentucky, Memphis, Palm Springs, and the Wisconsin Dells. We both learned from each other as our relationship grew, and we boldly leaped into uncharted territory (literally, in the case of our zip line adventure!)

On October 24, 2015, Andrea finally got to meet Matt's children, Henry, Joey, and Sophia. From that moment on, nothing has been the same. We have grown even closer together by adding in these three delightful (and challenging) kids, and we can't remember a time before the five of us were together.

Going back to the place of our first date, on March 15, 2016, Matt proposed to Andrea at Untitled. Sitting on the same couch where we spent that first evening together, Matt said "Just so you know, you're getting engaged tonight." Eventually, Andrea realized she needed to stop laughing and answer. Thankfully, the answer was "yes."

We have decided to celebrate our wedding together with you at a special place for our relationship - the Knob Creek House in Clermont, Kentucky. Our first road trip together was to Bourbon Country, and coming back to celebrate this next chapter in our life feels like the perfect pour.

Your Kentucky Experience

Whether you’re a bourbon enthusiast, a novice in the world of craft whiskey, or even a non-drinker, I’m sure you will agree that The Bourbon Trail is a very special place to visit and enjoy. The anchor point of the wedding weekend is Louisville, Kentucky, which is easily accessible driving or flying into SDF. We suggest you stay in downtown Louisville at the Marriott or Springhill Suites which we have arranged for special wedding rates for the weekend. Book early for the best rates.

On Friday night, September 30th, we plan to host a cocktail hour for anyone coming in town for the wedding. Check back here for more information as the date gets closer.

Our ceremony and reception will begin late afternoon Saturday, October 1st, at the Knob Creek House within a private area on the Jim Beam Distillery campus. We will have organized transportation from the designated hotels in downtown Louisville to take you on the 45-minute ride out to the Jim Beam Distillery and we’ll make sure you get back at the end of the night. If you prefer to drive, parking is readily available, but we ask you to please remember to designate a driver should you choose to drink.

Located in Loretto, Kentucky, the Jim Beam Distillery is the first official stop on The Bourbon Trail. The site of the wedding at the Knob Creek House is tucked away on private property, not accessible to the public, and this is the first wedding they have ever hosted! You will be part of history! The wedding will be outdoors, in a tent, and we plan to have plenty of comfortable seating, as well as dancing, and fun for all ages throughout the evening.

Before you come, do some research on Louisville and The Bourbon Trail! There’s so much to see and do in the area and we’re happy to provide suggestions on ways to fully enjoy the wedding weekend.
